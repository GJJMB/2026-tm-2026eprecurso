    Level sequence

      Drop the section files into assets/levels/sections/ and this level's file into assets/levels/levels/ (as <level id>.json). Then add "<level id>" to the sequence array in assets/levels/levels.json (if it isn't there already) to play it in-game.

      Campaign

        Levels here must each have exactly one spawn and one goal, summed across their sections — invalid ones show red. Saved campaigns appear in the game's main menu.

        Layer

        Foreground holds interactable Ground/Hazard tiles and entities — this is what the player collides with. Background holds purely decorative ground-equivalent sprites behind the action; it never collides and can't hold entities.

        Tiles

        Drag to paint. The highlighted bottom row is the ground baseline every section anchors to. Each brush below is its own sprite/color — add more in the Tileset section to paint several distinct-looking Ground/Hazard tiles in one section.

        Drag to paint decorative background tiles. These sit behind everything else and never block or collide with the player.

        Tileset (this section)

          Click a type to edit its color/sprite. "+ Add variant" makes a new brush with its own look (e.g. a second Ground sprite) that shows up as its own tool above and in the Type picker on a selected platform below. Each section keeps its own tileset, so different sections can look completely different even reused across levels.

          Entities

          Click an empty cell to place the selected entity (Spawn/Goal are singletons — placing a new one moves the old; Moving/enemies allow any number). Already-placed entities and platform tiles are listed in the right sidebar — select one there (or click its marker/tile here) to edit its properties. Selecting this tab switches the Tiles tab back to the Foreground layer, since entities can't live on the Background layer.

          Assets

          Assets belong to the campaign selected below in the Campaign panel — uploading one here bundles it with that campaign, not shared with any other. Kind (image vs. audio) is detected automatically from the file you pick.
